About

I am an undergraduate in Economics at The Ohio State University. My current projects explore how consumption structure reveals hidden constraints that traditional income or MPI measures miss. I am interested in theory that travels well to policy and in measurement that is transparent and replicable.

Research

Consumption Structure Index (CSI)

CSI proposes a fixed internal structure (weights and roles) with locally benchmarked thresholds to detect functional and symbolic constraints. A technical note with formal notation and examples is available upon request.

Planned empirical sketch

Illustrative calibration and robustness checks against alternative cutoff vectors. Pilot templates for reporting and visualization.

Reading notes

Summaries connecting Sen’s capability approach, Bourdieu on symbolic capital, and poverty measurement (FGT, MPI).
